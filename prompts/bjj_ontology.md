# BJJ Knowledge Ontology

This document defines the complete knowledge structure for Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) analysis, including positions, transitions, techniques, and scoring rules across multiple rulesets.

---

## Core Positions (18 Base Categories)

### 1. Guard (Bottom Position)
**Definition**: Bottom athlete has opponent between their legs, controlling distance and preventing advancement.

**Sub-positions**:
- **Closed Guard**: Legs locked around opponent's waist
- **Open Guard**: Legs not locked, using feet/shins to control
- **Half Guard**: One leg trapped between opponent's legs
- **Butterfly Guard**: Feet on opponent's hips, knees inside
- **De La Riva Guard**: One leg hooking opponent's leg from outside
- **X-Guard**: Both legs controlling one opponent's leg in X formation
- **Single Leg X**: One leg elevated, controlling opponent's leg
- **Z-Guard / Knee Shield**: One knee creating frame against opponent's chest

**Key characteristics**:
- Bottom athlete has defensive control
- Top athlete trying to pass
- Distance management critical

---

### 2. Mount (Top Position)
**Definition**: Top athlete sits on opponent's torso, both knees on ground or high mount position.

**Variations**:
- **Low Mount**: Sitting on hips/lower torso
- **High Mount**: Sitting on chest, knees near armpits
- **S-Mount**: One leg extended for armbar setup

**Key characteristics**:
- Dominant control position
- 4 points in ADCC
- High submission threat

---

### 3. Side Control (Top Position)
**Definition**: Top athlete chest-to-chest perpendicular to opponent, controlling from the side.

**Variations**:
- **Standard Side Control**: Chest pressure, crossface
- **Kesa Gatame**: Hip control, arm trapped
- **Reverse Kesa Gatame**: Facing opponent's legs
- **100 Kilos**: Heavy shoulder pressure on face

**Key characteristics**:
- Stable control position
- Transition hub to mount/north-south/back
- Pressure-based control

---

### 4. Back Control (Top Position)
**Definition**: Top athlete behind opponent with hooks in or body triangle.

**Variations**:
- **Hooks In**: Both heels hooked inside opponent's thighs
- **Body Triangle**: One leg triangled around opponent's torso
- **Single Hook**: One hook in, controlling with other leg
- **Seat Belt**: Over-under grip on upper body

**Key characteristics**:
- Most dominant position
- 4 points in ADCC
- Highest submission success rate

---

### 5. North-South (Top Position)
**Definition**: Top athlete's head near opponent's head, bodies aligned but opposite directions.

**Key characteristics**:
- Transitional position between side control and mount
- Limited submission options but strong control
- Often used for transitions

---

### 6. Knee on Belly (Top Position)
**Definition**: Top athlete places knee on opponent's belly/chest while other leg posts out.

**Key characteristics**:
- 2 points in ADCC
- High pressure, difficult to defend
- Easy transitions to mount/submissions

---

### 7. Turtle (Defensive Position)
**Definition**: Bottom athlete on hands and knees, protecting against back takes.

**Key characteristics**:
- Defensive position after failed guard or escape
- Common entry point for back control
- Transition position, not stable

---

### 8. Half Guard (Mixed Position)
**Definition**: One athlete's leg trapped between opponent's legs.

**Variations**:
- **Top Half Guard**: Passing position
- **Bottom Half Guard**: Guard-like control
- **Deep Half Guard**: Hip control from bottom
- **Lockdown**: Bottom athlete locks opponent's leg

**Key characteristics**:
- Neutral-to-advantageous depending on variation
- High transition rate
- Can be offensive or defensive

---

### 9. Standing (Neutral)
**Definition**: Both athletes on their feet, not engaged in ground work.

**Phases**:
- **Circling**: Movement, establishing grips
- **Grip Fighting**: Establishing control positions
- **Engaged**: Preparing for takedown or pull

---

### 10. Clinch (Standing)
**Definition**: Both athletes standing with established grips, body-to-body.

**Types**:
- **Over-Under**: One over, one under control
- **Collar Ties**: Neck control (no-gi)
- **Underhooks**: Both fighting for under control
- **Body Lock**: Arms locked around opponent

---

### 11. Takedown (Transition)
**Definition**: Active process of bringing opponent from standing to ground.

**Categories**:
- **Single Leg**: Attacking one leg
- **Double Leg**: Attacking both legs
- **Throw**: Hip toss, foot sweep
- **Trip**: Off-balancing with leg contact
- **Guard Pull**: Intentionally sitting to guard

---

### 12. Single Leg (Takedown Position)
**Definition**: Attacking one leg with various grip configurations.

**Phases**:
- **Penetration**: Lowering level, entering on leg
- **Elevation**: Lifting opponent's leg
- **Finish**: Driving through or tripping

---

### 13. Double Leg (Takedown Position)
**Definition**: Attacking both legs simultaneously.

**Phases**:
- **Level Change**: Dropping level rapidly
- **Penetration**: Driving through opponent's center
- **Finish**: Driving or lifting to complete takedown

---

### 14. Passing Guard (Transition)
**Definition**: Top athlete actively moving past bottom athlete's legs to side control or mount.

**Types**:
- **Over-Under Pass**: One leg over, one under
- **Knee Slice**: Cutting knee across opponent's legs
- **Leg Drag**: Pulling opponent's leg across body
- **Stack Pass**: Lifting hips and passing pressure
- **Toreando Pass**: Bullfighter-style leg control

---

### 15. Sweep (Transition)
**Definition**: Bottom athlete reversing position to achieve top control.

**Common Sweeps**:
- **Hip Bump**: From closed guard, bumping opponent forward
- **Scissor Sweep**: Using legs to off-balance
- **Butterfly Sweep**: Elevating with butterfly hooks
- **Pendulum Sweep**: Swinging leg motion

---

### 16. Submission Attempt (Action)
**Definition**: Actively applying a finishing technique (choke, joint lock).

**Categories**:
- **Chokes**: Blood chokes (cutting blood flow)
- **Arm Locks**: Armbar, kimura, americana
- **Leg Locks**: Heel hook, ankle lock, knee bar
- **Shoulder Locks**: Omoplata, crucifix

---

### 17. Scramble (Transition)
**Definition**: Chaotic transition period where both athletes are actively fighting for position.

**Characteristics**:
- Neither athlete has stable control
- Multiple position changes rapidly
- High transition rate
- Ends when someone establishes position

---

### 18. Unknown / Unclear
**Definition**: Position cannot be clearly identified due to angle, obscured view, or unusual configuration.

---

## Transition Types

### Guard → Mount
- **Common techniques**: Guard pass → side control → mount transition
- **Key indicators**: Legs cleared, chest pressure applied, knees to ground

### Guard → Back Control
- **Common techniques**: Turtle position → back take, failed guard pass → back take
- **Key indicators**: Opponent turns, hooks inserted

### Guard → Sweep
- **Common techniques**: Off-balancing, hip bump, butterfly elevation
- **Key indicators**: Bottom athlete gains top position

### Mount → Back Control
- **Common techniques**: Opponent turns to escape, take back
- **Key indicators**: Hooks inserted from mount

### Escape → Counter
- **Common techniques**: Frame and escape, reguard
- **Key indicators**: Space created, legs reinserted

## Scoring Systems

### ADCC Rules (Most Common No-Gi)
```
Takedown: 2 points
- Taking opponent from standing to ground with control
- Must establish control for 3 seconds

Knee on Belly: 2 points
- Knee on opponent's torso
- Other leg posted
- Must hold 3 seconds

Guard Pass: 3 points
- Passing opponent's legs to side control/mount/back
- Must establish control for 3 seconds

Mount: 4 points
- Sitting on opponent's torso
- Both knees on ground or high mount
- Must hold 3 seconds

Back Control: 4 points
- Behind opponent with hooks in or body triangle
- Must hold 3 seconds

Submission: Match ends
- Tap, verbal submission, or referee stoppage

Penalties:
- Stalling
- Illegal techniques
- Fleeing mat
```

### IBJJF (International Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Federation)
```
Takedown: 2 points
Guard Pass: 3 points
Knee on Belly: 2 points
Mount: 4 points
Back Control: 4 points
Sweep: 2 points (different from ADCC)

Advantages:
- Near submissions
- Near passes
- Near sweeps

Penalties:
- Stalling
- Illegal grips
- Leaving mat
```

### CJI (Craig Jones Invitational)
```
Similar to ADCC with modifications:
- Emphasis on submissions
- Modified penalty structure
- Submission-only in some brackets
```

### EBI (Eddie Bravo Invitational)
```
Submission only during regulation
Overtime rounds if no submission:
- Controlled position start
- Escape time measured
- Fastest escape or submission wins
```

## Technique Quality Indicators

### Position Control (1-5 scale)
- **5**: Dominant, stable, constant pressure
- **4**: Good control with minor adjustments
- **3**: Adequate control, some instability
- **2**: Weak control, opponent creating space
- **1**: Losing position, about to be escaped

### Transition Execution (1-5 scale)
- **5**: Textbook technique, smooth execution
- **4**: Clean execution with minor imperfections
- **3**: Successful but technical flaws
- **2**: Successful but sloppy
- **1**: Barely successful or failed

### Submission Threat (1-5 scale)
- **5**: Locked in, tap imminent
- **4**: Very close, small adjustments needed
- **3**: Clear submission attempt, defendable
- **2**: Submission setup, not yet threatening
- **1**: Fishing for submissions, no real threat

## Common Coaching Concepts

### Principles
- **Pressure**: Constant weight distribution
- **Frames**: Creating structure to maintain distance
- **Angles**: Off-center positioning for leverage
- **Grips**: Hand/arm placement for control
- **Base**: Stable foundation to prevent sweeps
- **Posture**: Upright position to prevent attacks

### Strategies
- **Play Guard**: Stay on bottom, work sweeps and submissions
- **Pass Guard**: Top position, work past legs
- **Top Control**: Maintain dominant position
- **Submission Hunting**: Actively seeking finishes
- **Points Strategy**: Win by accumulating points

### Schools of Thought
- **Danaher Death Squad**: Systematic approach, leg locks
- **10th Planet**: Rubber guard, unconventional positions
- **Gracie Barra**: Traditional, fundamental-based
- **Alliance**: Competition-focused, athletic style
- **Atos**: Technical precision, guard-centric
